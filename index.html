Monthly mileage pool — how it is calculated
Pool = average expected km, not maximum possible km
The logic
The 30 km per-ride cap is the maximum any single ride may use. Most Dubai trips are 12–18 km. The monthly pool is set at average expected distance × rides. A genuine daily commuter (JLT→DIFC = 12 km each way, 40 rides = 480 km) stays well inside a 700 km pool. Running every ride at maximum distance exhausts the pool before month-end — a natural brake protecting Moove's EV economics.

Model overview
How Fest Mobility works
Three parties. One clean model. No cars owned. No drivers employed. No capital at risk. Subscribers pay monthly — guaranteed EV at their door at their chosen time, every working day.
1
Company or individual subscribes
Chooses a package (Fest·ive / Fest·er / Fest·est). Registers home + office address. Picks their AM or PM time band. Pays one monthly fee in advance.
2
Fest books slots with Moove
Fest Mobility sends the subscriber's daily slot schedule to Moove. 75–100% of rides are pre-confirmed time slots — Moove plans their fleet accordingly.
3
Moove dispatches the EV
A dedicated Moove EV arrives at the subscriber's door at their chosen time. Driver is pre-assigned. Trip reported to RTA in real-time. No hailing, no waiting, no surge.
4
Fest retains 30%, remits 70%
At month-end Fest remits 70% of net subscription fees to Moove and retains 30% as commission. One VAT invoice to each subscriber. Repeat every month.
